The Pringles Cam

Intro

The Pringles Cam isn't a very big project at all. In fact, the current version took me no more than an hour to think of and make by hand. So, what is it?


It's what it sounds like: a Pringles can, but also a camera. I first thought of this by putting my phone vertically in a Pringles Can to hold it, but then a friend told me to stick it horizontally through the can, and so we had the Pringles Cam.


Originally, I just wanted something to record myself with in cubing competitions that wasn't super expensive (like a Go-Pro) or that would fall over easily (also like a Go-Pro). Pringles cans are pretty cheap (under $1 / can) and also have a hard time falling over, which is a perfect fit. Plus, it just looks funny to see a phone inside one. How I made mine

It's pretty obvious how you can make the Pringles Cam: cut slits and stick the phone inside. I'll show you how I made it here.


What you need:

  • A Pringles can
  • A phone. The width of your phone can't be taller than the can.
  • A cutting tool (preferably small). I used a utility knife because that's what I have.
  • (Recommended) Some electrical tape. Not everyone has this, but it's good for the cardboard and for your phone screen.


Start by putting the can on its side, then putting your phone on top of it. Outline the edge of that bottom part of your phone with a marker, and make the resulting outline a little taller (most phones have rounded corners).


I'm doing this one-handed. It wasn't fun.

The outline for my phone and case.


It's best to cut this slot out now. This is both so you know roughly how big the next one should be and to get an accurate place for the next slit (which will be across the diameter). I'd also suggest cutting down the height of the can instead of punching a hole with the blade because the latter can make the can collapse. I ended up cutting more than I thought I would.

The phone should fit in the slit snugly.


With the phone in, outline the inside of the can this time. You won't get the entire outline, but that's ok: you have the other slit to reference off of. Before outlining, try to make sure that your phone splits the can into two equal areas (in other words, make your phone as close to a diameter as possible).


You can check by looking down the slit you already cut and making sure the new outline is roughly lined up. Then cut the slit, but make sure both slits are lined up in terms of height.


I made my second slit a bit too wide towards the top, but that's ok. As long as the phone doesn't wiggle around or fall out (you can test this by shaking the can with the phone inside), it works. As you can see, it's already very close to done!


It's best to start marking at the top.

The phone should fit in the slit snugly.


You might notice that your phone case/phone is catching on the cardboard burrs. This is where electrical tape comes in. Electrical tape does two things: it adds grip and it creates a smoother surface.


To get rid of any sharp edges or big burrs, you can carefully file the slit edges down with a small file (if you have that) or run a knife blade back and forth on the rough edge. Then just cut your electrical tape to length and apply it, and we're done!


Done!

I like having a second set of angled slits for angled recording.
